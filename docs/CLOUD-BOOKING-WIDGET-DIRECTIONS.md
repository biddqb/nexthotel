# Cloud Booking Widget — 4 Visual Directions

Generated by /design-shotgun on 2026-04-27
Companion to: [CLOUD-DESIGN-DECISIONS.md](./CLOUD-DESIGN-DECISIONS.md)

These are 4 distinct visual directions for the public booking widget. Each is implementation-ready as a brief. Pick the one that matches the kind of hotel you're targeting — they're not just visual choices, they're positioning choices.

The information architecture is the same across all 4 (per design review): single-page progressive disclosure, brand bar → hero → date picker → rooms → booking form. What changes is voice, density, color use, photography, and motion.

---

## Direction A — "Calm and Trustworthy"

### Who this is for
- **Hotel type:** city business hotels, family hotels, mid-tier (~3-star) properties
- **Customer:** Vietnamese business travelers, families, repeat domestic guests
- **Positioning:** "We're the reliable, professional choice. Book direct with confidence."

### Mood
Calm. Professional. Reassuring. Like a well-run bank app — you don't notice the design, you just feel safe.

### Layout
- Constrained max-width 960px, generous left/right gutters
- Vertical rhythm: 32px between major sections, 16px within
- Rooms displayed as horizontal cards with photo on left (40% width), details on right (60%)
- Sticky header with brand bar + a small "Đặt phòng" CTA always visible

### Typography
- Hero headline: Be Vietnam Pro Bold, 36px, slate-900
- Section headers: Be Vietnam Pro Semibold, 20px, slate-900
- Body: Be Vietnam Pro Regular, 16px, slate-700, line-height 1.6
- Prices: Be Vietnam Pro Bold, 24px, slate-900 (NOT teal — keep teal for actions)
- Microcopy: 14px slate-500

### Color palette
```
Background:         #ffffff
Surface (cards):    #f8fafc (slate-50)
Borders:            #e2e8f0 (slate-200) hairline 1px
Primary text:       #0f172a (slate-900)
Secondary text:     #475569 (slate-600)
Muted:              #94a3b8 (slate-400)
Accent (CTA only):  #0d9488 (teal-600)
Accent hover:       #0f766e (teal-700)
Success:            #059669 (emerald-600)
Error:              #dc2626 (red-600)
```
**Rule:** teal appears ONLY on primary actions ("Tìm phòng", "Chọn", "Thanh toán"). Everything else is slate. This makes the action obvious.

### Photography
- One hero photo of the actual hotel exterior or signature room (no stock photos)
- Aspect 16:9, full bleed within content width, no overlay text
- Room photos: 4:3, soft natural light, no fisheye, no over-saturated edits
- No people in photos (avoids dating the photos when staff/guests change)

### Copy voice
Direct, professional, helpful. No exclamation marks. No "amazing", "incredible", "perfect". Just what is.

Examples:
- ✅ "Khách Sạn ABC · Đà Nẵng · 24 phòng"
- ❌ "Welcome to the amazing Khach San ABC!"
- ✅ "Phòng đôi tiêu chuẩn · 600.000₫ / đêm"
- ❌ "Spectacular Standard Double Room — Book Now!"

### Motion
- Subtle. Cards fade in 200ms when scrolled into view (one time, not on scroll up)
- CTA buttons: scale 1.02 on hover, no bounce
- Date picker: smooth open/close 150ms
- No parallax. No floating elements. No autoplay video.

### Trust signals
- Brand bar shows star rating + location
- Room cards show occupancy + amenity icons
- Booking summary always shows itemized total before payment
- "🔒 Thanh toán bảo mật bởi VNPay" + accepted card brand logos near pay button
- Simple cancellation policy stated before payment ("Huỷ miễn phí đến 24h trước nhận phòng")
- Reservation ID shown immediately after payment, plus email confirmation

### Mobile adaptations
- Cards stack vertically, photo on top, details below
- Date picker stacks
- Bottom-fixed CTA bar after a room is selected
- Touch targets 48px

### Fits this brand if
You want customers to feel "this hotel knows what it's doing." If your hotel has been around 10+ years, has consistent management, serves business travelers — this is the direction.

## Direction B — "Confident and Editorial"

### Who this is for
- **Hotel type:** boutique hotels, design-conscious properties, hotels with a strong story
- **Customer:** design-aware Vietnamese millennials, foreign tourists who pre-research
- **Positioning:** "We're a destination, not just a room. Book direct because we're worth it."

### Mood
Magazine. Editorial. Confident. Like reading Wallpaper or Monocle — you slow down to look.

### Layout
- Full-bleed hero, edge to edge, no constraint
- Type-driven hero: hotel name in 96px sans, subtitle in 24px serif italic, all text positioned bottom-left over a tinted photo
- Below the hero, content moves to constrained width 720px (narrower than A) for readability
- Rooms as full-width sections separated by horizontal rules, each with hero-style photo + serif headline
- Date picker is inline, no card around it

### Typography
- Hero headline: Be Vietnam Pro Black, 96px desktop / 56px mobile, slate-900
- Subtitle: Source Serif Pro Italic, 24px (introduce a serif for personality)
- Section headers: Be Vietnam Pro Bold, 32px, slate-900
- Body: Be Vietnam Pro Regular, 17px (slightly larger than A), line-height 1.7
- Prices: Be Vietnam Pro Light, 28px, slate-900 (lighter weight feels less aggressive)

### Color palette
```
Background:         #fefefe (warm off-white, not pure white)
Surface:            #f5f0e8 (cream, very subtle)
Borders:            #d4cfc4 (warm gray)
Primary text:       #1a1a1a (warm black, not pure black)
Secondary text:     #6b6960
Accent (CTA):       #0d9488 (teal-600) — kept for brand consistency
Accent dark:        #0f766e
```
The warm off-white + cream is the signature. Pure white feels clinical; warmth feels editorial.

### Photography
- Big hero photo. The hero IS the design.
- Aspect: full-bleed 16:9 desktop, 4:5 mobile (portrait crop)
- Photography style: natural light, slightly desaturated, more shadow than highlight, no people
- Room photos large (full content width), shown one at a time as user scrolls
- Each room has 2-3 photos in a horizontal scroll (no carousel arrows, just touch/scroll)

### Copy voice
Editorial, slightly poetic, restrained. Vietnamese tone is direct but with care.

Examples:
- ✅ "Khách Sạn ABC. Một góc Đà Nẵng từ năm 1998."
- ✅ "Phòng đôi. Cửa sổ hướng biển. Không TV. (Có wifi, đừng lo.)"
- ❌ "Welcome to Khách Sạn ABC, your perfect getaway!"

### Motion
- Hero text fade-up 400ms on load
- Image cross-fade for hotel photo gallery (3s interval, only if 3+ photos exist)
- Smooth scroll-to-section when CTA clicked
- Booking form slides in from right (320ms, ease-out)

### Trust signals
- "Từ năm 1998" or similar date as a small detail near brand
- Photo of the real owner/manager somewhere (signed by name) — adds humanity
- Awards or press mentions if real (Conde Nast, Lonely Planet, etc.) — small, footer-y
- VNPay trust signal same as Direction A but smaller, more integrated

### Mobile adaptations
- Hero crops to portrait 4:5
- Hero typography drops to 56px
- Single column throughout
- Hero photo + room photos same treatment

### Fits this brand if
Your hotel has a story. The owner cares about design. You serve guests who want experiences, not transactions. You compete on memorable, not cheap.

## Direction C — "Direct and Pragmatic"

### Who this is for
- **Hotel type:** budget hotels, mini-hotels, transit/airport hotels, business motels
- **Customer:** price-sensitive Vietnamese travelers, repeat business guests who book quickly
- **Positioning:** "No fluff. Rooms, prices, book. We don't waste your time."

### Mood
Boxy. Fast. Functional. Like Airbnb's first redesign or Booking.com without the dark patterns. The design says "we respect your time."

### Layout
- Tight max-width 720px, dense vertical rhythm (16px between sections)
- Room cards are compact horizontal strips: thumbnail (left, 80×80px) + room type + amenities + price + CTA on one line if possible
- Date picker is the FIRST thing visible (above any photos)
- Photos secondary — small thumbnails, expand on click

### Typography
- Hero headline: NONE — replaced with hotel name + 1-line description in 18px slate-900
- Section headers: Be Vietnam Pro Semibold, 16px (small)
- Body: Be Vietnam Pro Regular, 14px, slate-700, line-height 1.5 (tight)
- Prices: Be Vietnam Pro Bold, 20px, slate-900
- Everything is smaller than A and B

### Color palette
```
Background:         #ffffff
Surface:            #ffffff (no card backgrounds — borders only)
Borders:            #cbd5e1 (slate-300, slightly stronger than A)
Primary text:       #0f172a
Accent (CTA):       #0d9488 (teal-600)
Status success:     #059669
```
Almost no surface fills. Lines and text only. Looks like a well-designed spreadsheet — fast to scan.

### Photography
- Photos are utility, not hero
- Hotel exterior: small thumbnail in brand bar, ~40px tall
- Room photos: 80×80 thumbnails next to room name, expand on click
- No hero photo at all
- "View map" link instead of an embedded map

### Copy voice
Terse. Direct. Vietnamese tone is matter-of-fact.

Examples:
- ✅ "Khách Sạn ABC · 24 phòng · Đà Nẵng"
- ✅ "Phòng đơn · 1 giường · 350.000₫"
- ❌ "Our cozy single room offers a comfortable single bed"
- ❌ "Welcome!"

### Motion
- Almost none. Page is static.
- Date picker: instant open/close
- Hover states: underline only, no scale
- Loading: skeleton lines (no spinners)

### Trust signals
- Visible reservation count: "Đã có 1.243 đặt phòng tháng này" (if real)
- "Rẻ hơn Booking.com 15%" badge if hotel actually offers price-match (real claim only)
- VNPay logo + "Thanh toán bảo mật" — same as A
- "Phòng còn lại" indicator on each room: "Còn 2 phòng cho ngày này" (real-time)

### Mobile adaptations
- Single column, even tighter
- Date picker is 100% width, sticky at top of viewport
- Room cards collapse photo into a small avatar
- Bottom-fixed "Tiếp tục" CTA after room selected

### Fits this brand if
You're competing on price and speed of booking. Customer doesn't care about ambiance. They want to know "is there a room? what does it cost? can I book in 30 seconds?"

## Direction D — "Premium and Quiet"

### Who this is for
- **Hotel type:** 4-5 star resorts, boutique luxury, destination properties (Phu Quoc, Hoi An resort, Da Nang beachfront)
- **Customer:** affluent Vietnamese, foreign tourists comparing to Western booking sites
- **Positioning:** "Quality is in the details. We don't shout."

### Mood
Quiet. Confident. Refined. Like Aman or Six Senses websites — the design recedes so the property speaks.

### Layout
- Full-bleed hero photo with NO text overlay (text appears below)
- Hotel name in 32px, set on its own line below the hero (not on it)
- Generous max-width 1200px, lots of breathing room (48px between sections)
- Rooms shown as full-width sections, one per scroll-screen, with name + 2 photos + key details
- Booking form opens as a centered modal with frosted-glass background

### Typography
- Hotel name: serif Bodoni Moda or similar editorial display, 32px, weight 400
- Body: Be Vietnam Pro Regular, 18px (larger), line-height 1.8 (very generous)
- Section labels: 12px uppercase tracking 0.1em (small caps style)
- Prices: serif, 24px, weight 400

### Color palette
```
Background:         #fdfcf9 (warm bone)
Surface:            transparent (rely on photography)
Borders:            #e8e2d4 (warm)
Primary text:       #1f1d18
Secondary text:     #6b675e
Accent (CTA):       #2a4d4c (deep teal, more muted than #0d9488)
Accent hover:       #1f3938
```
Departures from desktop palette: warmer whites, deeper accent. Justified by audience expectation.

### Photography
- Hero: full-bleed, edge to edge, 16:9. Photo is the design.
- Photography style: golden hour, generous negative space within frame, no people, architectural
- 6+ photos minimum per room — gallery is a feature
- Map view available for resort properties (location is part of the value)

### Copy voice
Restrained, confident. Vietnamese in a literary register. English version mandatory for this tier.

Examples:
- ✅ "Tại bán đảo Sơn Trà, một ngôi nhà cũ được dựng lại."
- ✅ "Phòng nghỉ, sân riêng, vườn cau."
- ❌ "Stunning ocean views!"

### Motion
- Hero photo subtle Ken Burns (slow zoom, 20s loop)
- Section reveals on scroll, slow (600ms, ease-out)
- Booking modal: backdrop fade, modal slide-up, 400ms
- Premium feel from slowness — fast feels cheap here

### Trust signals
- Awards/press logos in footer (only if real — Tripadvisor Travelers' Choice, Conde Nast, etc.)
- A "concierge" contact option in addition to online booking ("Bạn cần đặt phòng riêng? Liên hệ với chúng tôi")
- Cancellation flexibility prominent ("Huỷ miễn phí đến 7 ngày trước")
- VNPay trust signal MORE subtle (small, gray, in the form footer rather than near the button)

### Mobile adaptations
- Hero crops portrait, parallax disabled
- Typography scales but stays generous (16px body min)
- Photos stay large
- Booking modal is full-screen on mobile

### Fits this brand if
Your hotel competes on prestige. Average daily rate >2M VND. Foreign tourist segment is significant. Direct booking is a status signal ("we don't need the OTAs, our guests come straight to us").

## How to pick

| Question | If A | If B | If C | If D |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Average price/night | 400-1.2M ₫ | 600-2M ₫ | 200-600 ₫ | 1.5M-10M+ ₫ |
| Customer mostly | Vietnamese business/family | Design-aware travelers | Price-sensitive | Affluent/foreign |
| Hotel age | 5+ years, established | 0-5 years, building identity | Any | 0+ years, premium positioning |
| Owner cares about | Reliability, ops | Story, design | Speed, efficiency | Prestige, details |
| Booking urgency | Medium | Low (research-first) | High | Low (research-first) |

**Most Vietnamese small hotels (10-60 rooms, 3-star, mid-priced) fit Direction A.** It's the safest default and aligns with desktop nextHotel's existing visual language. Pick A unless you have a specific reason to go B/C/D.

If you can only build one direction for v1: **A.** It's the broadest fit. You can offer B/C/D as paid customization later.

## Implementation handoff

Once you pick a direction, the implementation prompt is:

> "Implement the public booking widget at `/book/<branch-slug>` following Direction [X] from `docs/CLOUD-BOOKING-WIDGET-DIRECTIONS.md`. The implementer should not need to make significant visual decisions — between this doc, CLOUD-DESIGN-DECISIONS.md, and the existing tailwind config, every meaningful design choice is locked.
